# Household Arithmetic Fact Fluency

This pathway turns the household arithmetic brief into one contained route for daily work.

The goal is not to cover all of arithmetic at once. The goal is to move a learner through the smallest route that makes number facts reliable enough for later written maths.

## Pathway Order

1. [Readiness And Bonds Within 5](./playlists/readiness-and-bonds-within-5.md)
2. [Addition And Subtraction Facts To 10](./playlists/addition-and-subtraction-facts-to-10.md)
3. [Addition And Subtraction Facts To 20](./playlists/addition-and-subtraction-facts-to-20.md)
4. [Multiplication Facts Foundations](./playlists/multiplication-facts-foundations.md)
5. [Multiplication Facts Through 5](./playlists/multiplication-facts-through-5.md)
6. [Multiplication Facts Through 10](./playlists/multiplication-facts-through-10.md)

## Entry Points By Approximate Age

### Five-Year-Old

Start only at [Readiness And Bonds Within 5](./playlists/readiness-and-bonds-within-5.md).

Use this entry only when the child can count small groups reliably and can stay calm through a short ten-minute practice block. Stop after the readiness playlist and reassess before moving further.

### Seven-Year-Old

The default start is [Addition And Subtraction Facts To 10](./playlists/addition-and-subtraction-facts-to-10.md).

Drop back to the readiness playlist only if number bonds within 5 are still slow or shaky.

### Ten-Year-Old

Begin with a quick baseline check from [Multiplication Facts Foundations](./playlists/multiplication-facts-foundations.md).

If the learner is already secure on `2`, `5`, and `10` facts, move forward. If not, start there. If addition and subtraction facts through 20 are still weak, route back to [Addition And Subtraction Facts To 20](./playlists/addition-and-subtraction-facts-to-20.md).

## Parent Delivery Rules

- Keep sessions to 10 to 15 minutes.
- Prefer accuracy first and speed second.
- Use oral recall, paper practice, and checks for different jobs.
- Repeat weak facts often, but do not repeat the entire route when only a few facts are weak.

## Exit Conditions

The pathway is secure when the learner can:

- answer mixed addition and subtraction facts through 20 with only occasional hesitation
- answer mixed multiplication facts through `10 x 10` with strong accuracy
- use inverse relationships sensibly, especially for subtraction and division
