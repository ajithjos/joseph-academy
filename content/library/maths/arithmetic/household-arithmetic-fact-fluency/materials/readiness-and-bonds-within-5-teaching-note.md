# Readiness And Bonds Within 5 Teaching Note

Adult guide: let the learner look at the lesson note first, then use these prompts with counters, buttons, or fingers.

## Session purpose

The learner is not just memorising answers. The learner is building the idea that a small whole has parts inside it.

## What to use

- counters, buttons, coins, or dry beans
- fingers
- a small whiteboard if helpful

## Teaching sequence

1. Show a group of up to 5 objects for two seconds, then hide it and ask, `How many did you see?`
2. Split `5` into two parts and say the whole fact aloud, such as `3 and 2 make 5`.
3. Ask the inverse prompt straight away, such as `If 3 and 2 make 5, what is 5 take away 2?`
4. Mix in a few tiny addition prompts and a few tiny subtraction prompts so the learner hears the same numbers in different forms.

## What to watch for

- good sign: the learner starts answering before recounting every object
- warning sign: the learner can repeat `2 and 3 make 5` but cannot answer `5 - 2`
- warning sign: the learner answers by guessing faster instead of reasoning better

## Correction rule

Do not say `No, that is wrong` and move on. Rebuild the whole with the objects, show the two parts, then ask the matching fact again.

Stop before fatigue appears. A short calm session is better than a long frustrated one.
