# Addition And Subtraction Facts To 20 Lesson Note

Make `10` first. Then finish the fact.

Facts through `20` are easier when you stop treating them as long counting jobs.

## The main idea

Look for a bond you already know inside the bigger fact.

If you can make `10`, or see how far a number is from `10`, you can solve many teen-number facts without counting one by one.

## Use an anchor

- `8 + 7 = 8 + 2 + 5 = 15`
- `9 + 6 = 9 + 1 + 5 = 15`
- `13 - 5 = 13 - 3 - 2 = 8`
- `16 - 7 = 16 - 6 - 1 = 9`

Read those like this:

- `I bridge to 10 first.`
- `Then I finish the fact.`

## Worked examples

### Example 1: `8 + 7`

- `8 needs 2 to make 10`
- split `7` into `2 and 5`
- `8 + 2 = 10`
- `10 + 5 = 15`
- so `8 + 7 = 15`

### Example 2: `15 - 7`

- from `15`, take `5` to reach `10`
- there are `2` more to take away
- `10 - 2 = 8`
- so `15 - 7 = 8`

## Missing-number thinking

- `__ + 6 = 14` means `6 and what make 14?`
- `15 - __ = 8` means `what part was taken away?`

Do not read the blank as a trick. Read it as the missing part of the whole.

## Fact families around 20

If `12 + 8 = 20`, then:

- `8 + 12 = 20`
- `20 - 12 = 8`
- `20 - 8 = 12`

## Good learner self-talk

- `I can bridge through 10.`
- `I know the whole and I need the missing part.`
- `This subtraction belongs to the same family as the addition.`

## Quick spoken practice

1. `9 + 5 =`
2. `14 - 6 =`
3. `__ + 7 = 20`
4. `13 + 6 =`
5. `17 - 8 =`
6. `20 - __ = 4`

Look for a known bond inside the bigger fact. Use that anchor instead of counting every step.
