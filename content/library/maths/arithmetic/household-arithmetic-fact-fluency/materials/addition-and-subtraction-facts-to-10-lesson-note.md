# Addition And Subtraction Facts To 10 Lesson Note

This lesson is about seeing a whole number as two parts that fit together.

When you know the pair, you do not have to count from the start every time. You can look at the whole, remember the parts, and answer quickly.

## What addition and subtraction are doing

- Addition puts parts together to make a whole.
- Subtraction starts with the whole and asks for a part.
- A fact family is one set of numbers that tells the same story in different ways.

If the whole is `10`, then `7` and `3` are not random numbers. They are two parts that fit inside `10`.

## Bonds to 10

Learn these until they feel obvious:

- `0 and 10 make 10`
- `1 and 9 make 10`
- `2 and 8 make 10`
- `3 and 7 make 10`
- `4 and 6 make 10`
- `5 and 5 make 10`

Say them both ways as well:

- `9 and 1 make 10`
- `8 and 2 make 10`
- `7 and 3 make 10`
- `6 and 4 make 10`

## One story, four facts

If `7 + 3 = 10`, then the same story also gives you:

- `3 + 7 = 10`
- `10 - 7 = 3`
- `10 - 3 = 7`

Read that family like this:

- `7` and `3` make the whole `10`
- if the whole is `10` and one part is `7`, the missing part is `3`
- if the whole is `10` and one part is `3`, the missing part is `7`

## Worked examples

### Example 1: `8 + 2`

Ask: what whole do these parts make?

- `8 and 2 make 10`
- so `8 + 2 = 10`

### Example 2: `10 - 6`

Ask: what part is missing from `10` if one part is `6`?

- `6 and 4 make 10`
- so `10 - 6 = 4`

### Example 3: `9 - 1`

Ask: what goes with `1` to make `9`?

- `8 and 1 make 9`
- so `9 - 1 = 8`

## Good learner self-talk

Use one calm sentence instead of counting in ones.

- `I know the bond to 10.`
- `I know the whole, so I can find the missing part.`
- `This subtraction belongs to the same addition family.`

## Common mistakes

- Treating every subtraction fact as brand new.
- Knowing `5 + 5` but not the other pairs to `10`.
- Counting from `1` even when the bond is already known.

If that happens, stop and ask: what are the two parts of the whole?

## Quick spoken practice

Say the answer without writing first.

1. `6 + 4 =`
2. `10 - 2 =`
3. `7 + 1 =`
4. `9 - 4 =`
5. `__ + 3 = 10`
6. `10 - 8 =`

## Exit idea

If you can say a bond and then its matching subtraction straight away, the fact family is starting to stick.

Example:

- `4 and 6 make 10`
- `10 - 4 = 6`
- `10 - 6 = 4`
